STL Sequence Containers

The STL sequence containers are:

  1. vector<>
  2. deque<>
  3. list<>
C++ arrays are also sequence containers but without methods.

Sequence Container Overview

Container Properties

The order of the objects in sequence containers is under the control of the application.

Different sequence container types have different internal organisations to meet different needs but they share a large subset of common methods.

  1. Prefer deque<> unless there is good reason to use one of the other container types.
  2. Prefer vector<> when compatibility with C/C++ arrays is required.
  3. Pointers to C/C++ arrays can be used as iterators with most algorithms but arrays have no container methods and cannot be resized at run-time.
  4. Prefer list<> when it offers better performance and you do not need array syntax.
  5. Prefer a container adaptor when one of these offers the required semantics and you do not need iterators or algorithms.

  1. list<> may be a better choice when you need to insert/erase objects from the middle, rather than the ends, of the container.
  2. list<> may be a better choice when you need to change the order of objects and the number of objects is large.
  3. list<> may be a better choice when you can use the list specific methods extensively.

Common Container Methods

The sequence containers have a great many methods in common.

The sequence container construction methods are the to-be-expected:

  • constructor,
  • destructor and
  • operator=.

The sequence container iterator methods are:

  • begin(),
  • end(),
  • rbegin() and
  • rend().

The sequence container capacity methods are:

  • empty(),
  • size(),
  • max_size() and
  • resize().

The access methods supported by all sequence container types are:

  • front(),
  • back().

The modifier methods supported by all sequence container types are:

  • assign(),
  • insert(),
  • erase(),
  • swap(),
  • clear(),
  • push_back() and
  • pop_back().

Container Specific Methods

vector<> offers two additional capacity methods:

  • capacity() and
  • reserve().

list<> does not offer the random access methods: at() and operator[].

vector<> does not offer the modifier methods:

  • push_front() and
  • pop_front().

list<> offers the additional modifier methods:

  • merge(),
  • remove(),
  • remove_if(),
  • reverse(),
  • sort(),
  • splice() and
  • unique().
