STL Numerics

Complex Numbers

The <complex> header file provides a template class, functions and operators for complex numbers and overloads for transcendental functions.

The template class declaration is: 

    template< class T > class complex;

The STL provides three specialisations: 

    complex< float >
    complex< double >
    complex< long double >
The effect of instantiating the complex<> class for any other type is unspecified.

Internally, the complex<> class has one data member, which is an array of dimension 2. This ensures binary compatibility with the C-language complex number types.

Thus internally, complex numbers are stored in Cartesian form with '[real,imaginary]' parts. Template functions provide for polar co-ordinate interpretation.

Constructors

The primary constructor is: 

    complex ( const T& real = T(), const T& im = T() );
so the default constructor yields the origin of the complex plane.

The copy constructors allow for conversion between the three complex<> specialisations.

Member and Non Member Functions

The member functions (excluding operators) are:

  1. real() real part of the complex number
  2. imag() imaginary part of the complex number
These may be used to set and get the real and imaginary parts.

The non-member functions (excluding operators) are:

  1. real() real part of the complex number
  2. imag() imaginary part of the complex number
  3. abs() polar magnitude of the complex number
  4. arg() polar phase angle of the complex number
  5. norm() the squared or normalised magnitude
  6. conj() complex conjugate of complex number
  7. polar() complex number from polar co-ordinates
  8. proj() complex projection onto the Riemann sphere
These take a complex<> argument.

Operators

Most operators accept a scalar argument (of arbitrary numeric type). A scalar is taken to be a complex number with no imaginary part.

The member operators are:

  1. operator= assignment from one complex<> to another
  2. operator+= plus equals
  3. operator-= minus equals
  4. operator/= divide equals
  5. operator*= times equals
All but the first a accept scalar parameter.

The non-member operators are:

  1. operator+ unary plus
  2. operator- unary minus
  3. operator+ binary plus
  4. operator- binary minus
  5. operator* binary times
  6. operator/ binary divide
  7. operator== binary equals
  8. operator!= binary not equals
All support mixed complex/scalar arithmetic.

The i/o stream operators are:

  1. operator<< insert a complex number into an output stream
  2. operator>> extract a complex number from an input stream

Extraction accepts 'real', '(real)' and '(real,imag)'. Insertion always produces '(real,imag)'. They are not locale aware.

Transcendental Functions

These are the complex versions of transcendental functions defined in <cmath>.

  1. cos() cosine of a complex number
  2. cosh() hyperbolic cosine of a complex number
  3. exp() exponential of a complex number
  4. log() natural logarithm of a complex number
  5. log10() common logarithm of a complex number
  6. pow() power of a complex number
  7. sin() sine of a complex number
  8. sinh() hyperbolic sine of a complex number
  9. sqrt() square root of a complex number
  10. tan() tangent of a complex number
  11. tanh() hyperbolic tangent of a complex number
  12. acos() arc cosine of a complex number
  13. acosh() arc hyperbolic cosine of a complex number
  14. asin() arc sine of a complex number
  15. asinh() arc hyperbolic sine of a complex number
  16. atan() arc tangent of a complex number
  17. atanh() arc hyperbolic tangent of a complex number
These do not accept scalar arguments.
